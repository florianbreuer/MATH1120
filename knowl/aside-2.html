Factorials.

Here \(n! = 1\cdot 2 \cdot 3 \cdots n\) is \(n\) factorial. It grows really fast:
\begin{align*} 0! \amp = 1\\ 1! \amp = 1\\ 2! \amp = 2\\ 3! \amp = 6\\ 4! \amp = 24\\ 5! \amp = 120\\ 6! \amp = 720\\ \amp \vdots \\ 10! \amp = 3628800 \end{align*}
The factorial satifies the useful property that \((n+1)! = (n+1)n!\text{,}\) meaning that fractions with factorials in the numerator and denominator will often allow lots of cancellation.
