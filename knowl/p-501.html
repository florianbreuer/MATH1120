The temperature at the point \((x,y,z)\) is given by the function
\begin{equation*} T(x,y,z) = 200e^{-x^2-3y^2-9z^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  1. Find the rate of change of temperature at the point \(P = (2,-1,2)\) in the direction \(\overrightarrow{PQ}\) where \(Q = (3,-3,3)\text{.}\)
  2. In which direction does the temperature increase the fastest at \(P\text{?}\)
  3. Find the maximum rate of increase at \(P\text{.}\)
