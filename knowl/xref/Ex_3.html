Example 9.4.

It has been observed that an object cools at a rate proportional to the difference between the objectâ€™s temperature and ambient temperature. Write down an equation that conveys this observation.
Solution.
Let \(T(t)\) be the temperature of the object at time \(t\) after we begin the observation. Also let \(T_a\) be the ambient temperature. Then
\begin{equation*} \frac{dT}{dt}=-k(T-T_a) \end{equation*}
where \(k\) is a constant.
