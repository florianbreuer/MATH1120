Theorem 18.18.

To calculate the determinant of the \(n \times n \) matrix \(A \text{:}\)
  • Row reduce \(A \) to an equivalent upper triangular matrix, \(B \text{,}\) noting that;
  1. If matrix \(B \) is obtained from matrix \(A \) by interchanging \(2 \) rows then
    \begin{equation*} \det(B)=-\det(A). \end{equation*}
  2. If matrix \(B \) is obtained from matrix \(A \) by multiplying a row of \(A \) by a scalar \(k \) then
    \begin{equation*} \det(B)=k\det(A). \end{equation*}
  3. If matrix \(B \) is obtained from matrix \(A \) by adding a multiple of one row of \(A \) to another then
    \begin{equation*} \det(B)=\det(A). \end{equation*}
  • Find the determinant of \(B \) by finding the product of the entries on the main diagonal of \(B \text{.}\)
