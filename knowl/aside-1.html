Faster multiplication.

There are actually multiplication algorithms which are significantly faster than long multiplication. Long multiplication of of two \(n\)-digit numbers will typically take \(n^2\) single-digit multiplications (and another \(n\) \(n\)-digit additions). The fastest known algorithm 1  is due to David Harvey and Joris van der Hoeven, and requires a number of steps proportional to \(n\ln(n)\text{,}\) which for large value of \(n\) is a significant improvement.
