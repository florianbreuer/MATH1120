Solution 3.4.1.

  1. Since we can calculate \(f\) for all values of \(x\) and \(y\) the domain of \(f\) is \(\mathbb{R}^2\)
  1. \(\displaystyle f(3,1)=1-3\times 1=-2\)
  2. \(\displaystyle f(\frac{1}{2},-\sqrt{5})=-\sqrt{5}+\frac{1}{2}\times\sqrt{5}=-\frac{\sqrt{5}}{2}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle f(1,3)=3-1\times 3=0\)
