Theorem 20.10.

Consider the system of n coupled first-order linear differential equations
\begin{equation*} \dot{\mathbf{x}} = A \mathbf{x} \end{equation*}
where \(A\) is a real \(n \times n\) matrix with \(n\) distinct eigenvalues \(\lambda_i\text{,}\) \(i = 1,2,\ldots,n\text{.}\) Then the general solution to this system is
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}(t) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} C_i e^{\lambda_i t} \mathbf{v_i} \end{equation*}
where \(\mathbf{v_i}\) is the eigenvector corresponding to eigenvalue \(\lambda_i\text{.}\)
