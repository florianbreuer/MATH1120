Exercise 1.
At time \(t\) the population density of the Striped Dingbat is modelled by the function \(P(t)\text{.}\) This function satisﬁes the DE
\begin{equation*} \frac{dP}{dt}=kP(C-P) \end{equation*}
where \(k\) and \(C\) are positive constants.
  1. Find all steady-state solutions to this DE.
  2. By considering the direction ﬁeld, explain how this model predicts the long-term Striped Dingbat population.
