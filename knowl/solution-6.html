Solution 2.10.b.1.
We compute
\begin{align*} \sum_{k=0}^\infty x^k \amp = \lim_{N\rightarrow\infty} \sum_{k=0}^N x^k \\ \amp = \lim_{N\rightarrow\infty} \frac{1-x^{N+1}}{1-x} \\ \amp = \frac{1}{1-x} \end{align*}
since \(x^N \rightarrow 0\) as \(N\rightarrow\infty\) because \(|x| \lt 1\text{.}\)
